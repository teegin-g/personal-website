## About

I’m a Business Development analyst at Continental Resources with prior experience in reservoir and production analytics. I graduated from Oklahoma City University with a degree in Economics and Math. My background is in economics, math, and statistical modeling, and most of my work revolves around building applications, databases and tools to tackle complex financial and engineering problems. I have a breadth of experience across industries —finance, research, and now upstream E&P — where I've worked on projects ranging from simple market analysis to full blown analytical applications and ML models. I enjoy leveraging my quantitative background to discover novel ways to tackle a challenge whether it's a simple regression on FRED data or training an ensemble of models to predict performance on thousands of wells. Before Continental, I did economic research at Oklahoma City University, worked on portfolio and market analysis in financial services, and won first place in Manhattan College’s Business Analytics Competition for a project on COVID policy effectiveness using Bayesian and probabilistic methods. I also served as VP of the Lambda Chi Alpha fraternity, competed on the debate team and was on the board of the Meinder's Leadership Fellows. I’m especially interested in work that blends technical and quantitative rigor with high value economic decisions.
